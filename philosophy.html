The Philosophy of Carl Jung

Jung's philosophy is based on the belief that humans are not only biological and social beings but also possess a deeply spiritual dimension. For Jung, the goal of life is not merely adaptation or happiness, but the unfolding of the true self – a process he called "individuation."

Central to this is the collective unconscious: a shared psychic background of all humanity, populated by archetypes – universal symbols such as the Hero, the Mother, or the Shadow. These influence dreams, myths, art, and personal development.

Jung emphasized the necessity of confronting the dark sides of the self – the "Shadow" – in order to become whole. The inner contrasexual dimension (Anima/Animus) and the spiritual realm also play a central role in his philosophy.

His approach weaves psychology, philosophy, religion, and art into a holistic understanding of the human being. For Jung, true healing is not a technical process but a deep inner transformation toward authenticity and meaning.
